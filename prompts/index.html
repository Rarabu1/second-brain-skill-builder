Module 04 · Technique

Prompts that work.

Most prompts fail before the AI reads them. Not because the model is wrong. Because the request doesn't give it enough to do the task right.

The gap

Vague in, vague out.

The same prompt from two different people produces two different outputs. Not because Claude is inconsistent. Because the same words mean different things depending on what you're actually trying to do.

Before
Write me a meeting summary.
After
You're a concise executive writer. Summarize this transcript into 5 bullet points of decisions made and 3 open action items. Format as a Slack message, under 200 words. Skip anything that's just socializing. Here's the transcript: [paste]

The difference is 47 words. One gives the AI a job. The other gives it a guess.

The rule

Every vague word in your prompt is a decision you're handing to the AI. Specificity scales directly with output quality.

Before you type

Four questions.

Not all of these are relevant every time. But when a draft misses, it's usually because one of them got skipped.

  1. 01
    What does done look like?

    Not the task: the output. "Give me 5 bullets, each under 20 words, one decision per bullet" is a different instruction than "write me a summary." Both technically ask for the same thing. One of them works.

  2. 02
    Who's reading this?

    The AI calibrates tone, length, and what to include around the audience. "For my director" changes the output more than almost anything else you could add.

  3. 03
    What does the AI not know that matters?

    Background context, domain specifics, or constraints that aren't obvious from the task. Think about what you'd say walking a colleague through it right before you handed it off.

  4. 04
    What would you reject?

    Think backwards. Name one or two outputs you'd immediately discard. That boundary is often the most useful constraint you can write.

The framework

Six layers.

Every good prompt is assembled from some combination of six things. Not all six, every time. Role is usually optional. Objective is not.

Layer What it does Required Example
Role Tells Claude who to be for this task No "You're a direct executive editor."
Objective States what needs to be done Yes "Summarize this transcript into 5 decisions and 3 action items."
Context Supplies background the AI doesn't have No "This is for a director who wasn't in the meeting."
Structure Shapes the output format No "Format as a Slack message, under 200 words."
Constraints Names what to exclude or avoid No "Skip socializing and meta-commentary."
Data The material to work with No "Here's the transcript: [paste]"
In practice

Three assembled prompts.

Each one labeled by layer so the pattern is visible. This is what The Brief produces in annotated view.

Task Email draft
// Role You're a direct, professional communicator who writes concise emails. // Objective Draft a follow-up email to a vendor after a meeting where we agreed to pilot their product. // Context The meeting was positive. They offered a 30-day trial. I need to confirm scope and get a contract started. // Structure Three paragraphs max. Include a subject line. // Constraints No filler phrases. No "I hope this email finds you well."
Task Research summary
// Objective Summarize the key arguments for and against mandatory AI disclosure requirements in financial services. // Context Writing a policy brief for a non-technical audience. Assume they know finance but not AI regulation. // Structure Two sections: arguments for, arguments against. Three bullets each, one sentence per bullet. // Constraints No citations needed. No academic language.
Task Document review
// Role You're a senior policy editor who values precision and plain language. // Objective Review this draft and flag any claims that are overstated, vague, or that could embarrass the organization. // Context This is going to a regulator who will be looking for precision. Any hedge or overstatement will be noticed. // Constraints Don't rewrite. Annotate with line numbers and a one-sentence explanation per flag. // Data [paste document]
